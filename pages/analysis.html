There is a negative relationship between APG and Season.

1999-2000 NBA season was the best in terms of average assists per game by a player. Overtime we see average APG of a player dropping gradually, this could be because older teams are more dominant in passing, or, prephaps the focus from passing is shifting.





1999-2003 had more dominant passers than newer Seasons.

A high performer in passing is one who has a high assist to turnover ratio, this way people who have a high assist average and a high turnover average do not get included. The results show that older seasons have more high performers than newer seasons. Thus older seasons having more dominant passers.





1999-2000 NBA season was the best in terms of average points per game by a team.

Older seasons performed better than newer seasons on most cases in average ppg of teams from 1999-2007. This conflicted with our hypothesis because we expected newer season teams to be better scorers.





1999-2003 had more dominant defenders than newer Seasons.

A high performer in defence is one who has high steal average or high block average. Older seasons had more high performers than newer seasons, showing older seasons were more defensive minded.





1999-2000 NBA season was the most defence minded Season.

In this case defence is the accumulation of steals and blocks of a team from a season. There is a negative trend over time, showing perhaps focus is shifting from defence over time. It could also be that older seasons teams are just better at defence.
