What are the differences in performance of players and teams across seasons from 1999-2007?





Hypothesis

We expect the older seasons to perform better in defence and sharing the ball, whereas the newer seasons to be better at scoring. We made the assumption to bin older seasons into 1999-2003 and newer seasons into 2003-2007. The is to due to influences of coach Mike d'antoni on the NBA around 2003 which put more emphasis on scoring. He popularised offensive plays like running and gunning, pick and rolls, and isolations.





There is a negative relationship between APG and Season.

1999-2000 NBA season was the best in terms of average assists per game by a player. Overtime we see average APG of a player dropping gradually , not by much, but dropping nonetheless. This could be due to an excessive use of pick and roll plays, which are offense based but still involve passing, but not as much as other plays. Hence a small drop in assists per game of a player.





1999-2003 had more dominant passers than newer Seasons.

A high performer in passing is one who has a high assist to turnover ratio, this way people who have a high assist average and a high turnover average do not get included. The results show that older seasons have more high performers than newer seasons. Thus older seasons having more dominant passers.





1999-2000 NBA season was the best in terms of average points per game by a team.

Older seasons performed better than newer seasons on most cases in average points per game of teams from 1999-2007. This conflicted with our hypothesis because we expected newer season teams to be better scorers.





1999-2003 had more dominant defenders than newer Seasons.

A high performer in defence is one who has high steal average or high block average. Older seasons had more high performers than newer seasons, showing older seasons were more defensive minded. Another reason for this result could be the banning of hand checking in 2003.





1999-2000 NBA season was the most defence minded Season.

In this case defence is the accumulation of steals and blocks of a team from a season. It is clear that older season teams are better at defence than newer teams. One strong reason for this result is there was a rule change which banned hand checking in 2003, which in turn made defence harder and could explain this result.




Conclusion

We suspected newer seasons to be better at scoring than older seasons, however this was not the case. Older seasons were better in scoring, passing the ball and defence. Therefore our hypothesis is partially valid. A reason why older seasons are better at defence is due to a rule change curtailing hand checking, which would have made defence harder for players. A reason for why older seasons could be better at passing might be due to different play strategies being employed. Finally, older season teams are better at scoring because the high performers in scoring may be spread out in teams, as opposed to being skewed to certain teams in newer seasons, however further analysis would be needed to prove this.
